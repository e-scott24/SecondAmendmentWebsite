Pros & Cons of the Second Amendment

Introduction

The Second Amendment is a highly polarizing topic in today's society.

Many Americans and politicians believe that "the Amendment's phrase 'the right of the people to keep and bear Arms' creates an individual constitutional right to possess firearms." This side refers to an individual rights theory which asserts that the "Constitution restricts legislative bodies from prohibiting firearm possession, or at the very least, the Amendment renders prohibitory and restrictive regulation presumptively unconstitutional" (Legal Information Institute).

Many other Americans and politicians "point to the prefatory language 'a well regulated Militia' to argue that the Framers intended only to restrict Congress from legislating away a state's right to self-defense." This side refers to a collective rights theory which "asserts that citizens do not have an individual right to possess guns and that local, state, and federal legislative bodies therefore possess the authority to regulate firearms without implicating a constitutional right" (Legal Information Institute).

Supreme Court decisions throughout history, and especially in recent years, have changed the way courts, politicians, and citizens perceive and consider the Second Amendment.

Because of the political and polarizing nature of the Second Amendment, it is difficult to list any truly unbiased pros and cons. Which side of the argument you stand with will typically determine what you believe and agree/disagree with regarding the Second Amendment. The following sections will explain some arguments from each side.

Proponent Arguments

Proponents of the Second Amendment argue for loose or little-to-no gun control measures.

Gun control is the "regulation of the selling, owning, and use of guns" (Merriam-Webster). Gun control varies across the nation, with some states being much stricter than others.

Some states allow any citizen who can legally possess and carry a gun to do so without the need for a permit/license, while others require a permit/license to be obtained first. Read more about this in the State Information page.

There are several arguments for the Second Amendment, including (Legal Vidhiya):

  • Self-Defense
  • Hunting & Recreational Shooting
  • Protection Against Tyranny
  • Crime Deterrence
  • Constitutional Right

The main argument for the Second Amendment is the right to self-defense, which the Supreme Court confirmed in the case District of Columbia v. Heller. Many argue that when law-abiding citizens are allowed to carry guns for self-defense and the protection of others, it deters criminals. The Second Amendment also allows the ownership and use of firearms for hunting purposes and for recreational shooting (such as at a gun range), which many argue does not present any harm to citizens. Many argue that the phrase "being necessary to the security of a free State" in the Second Amendment points to the protection against government tyranny; citizens have the right to own firearms to protect themselves and other citizens from a potential tyrannical government. Lastly, many simply argue that owning and carrying firearms is a constitutional right, because of the Second Amendment, and that it should not be "infringed upon" in any way.

Gun Control Act of 1968

The Gun Control Act came after the assassinations of President John Kennedy, Attorney General Robert Kennedy and Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr.

The Gun Control Act "imposes stricter licensing and regulation on the firearms industry, establishes new categories of firearms offenses, and prohibits the sale of firearms and ammunition to felons and certain other prohibited persons" (ATF.gov).

The Act also "imposes the first Federal jurisdiction over "destructive devices," including bombs, mines, grenades and other similar devices" (ATF.gov).

Brady Handgun Violence Prevention Act of 1993

The Brady Act amended the Gun Control Act of 1968.

The act "imposed as an interim measure a waiting period of 5 days before a licensed importer, manufacturer, or dealer may sell, deliver, or transfer a handgun to an unlicensed individual" (ATF.gov), required "Federal Firearms Licensees (FFL) to request background checks on prospective firearm transferees," and "required the U.S. Attorney General to establish the National Instant Criminal Background Check System (NICS)" (FBI.gov).

According to the FBI, the National Instant Criminal Background Check System (NICS) process is as follows:

  1. "When a person tries to buy a firearm, the seller, known as a Federal Firearms Licensee (FFL), contacts NICS electronically or by phone."
  2. "The prospective buyer fills out the ATF form, and the FFL relays that information to the NICS."
  3. "The NICS staff performs a background check on the buyer. That background check verifies the buyer does not have a criminal record or isn't otherwise ineligible to purchase or own a firearm."
  4. There have been more than 500 million NICS checks since 1998, and more than two million denials (FBI.gov).

I believe that gun control measures only hinder a law-abiding citizen's right to self-defense because gun control measures do nothing to prevent criminals from obtaining illegal firearms. Criminals are called criminals for a reason, they do not obey the law and will always find ways around it.

I believe one of, if not the biggest issue regarding firearms in the United States is that people are ingorant, unaware, and/or improperly trained with firearms. I believe 100% that a firearm should be in someone's home for protecting their family. This clearly presents a danger if a child gets their hands on a firearm, but this is where the responsibility of the parent comes in. The parent should teach their children gun safety, and should NEVER leave a loaded firearm easily accessible to an unsupervised young child. When children are raised to be scared of guns and are not given proper gun safety lessons, they grow into adults not having a clue how a firearm works. I believe the biggest danger of firearms is one being in the hands of someone who has no idea how to safely and properly handle it.

Guns are not necessarily dangerous by themselves; they become dangerous when they are being handled by people who have no knowledge of firearms and no sense of gun safety. While not all Second Amendment advocates may agree with me, I believe that gun safety training should be required.
